## General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a passphrase like "BlueSky@VIT2025" — mix letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across platforms.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — Gmail, GitHub, LinkedIn, and banking apps. This adds a second layer of security even if your password is stolen.

3. **Never click on suspicious links** received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email — even if they appear to come from known contacts. Always verify by calling the sender directly.

4. **Keep your devices and apps updated.** Software updates often include critical security patches that protect against known vulnerabilities.

5. **Use only trusted Wi-Fi networks.** Avoid logging into banking or email on public Wi-Fi at cafes or bus stations. Use a VPN if necessary.

6. **Review app permissions regularly.** Deny unnecessary permissions — a flashlight app does not need access to your contacts or camera.

---

## Financial / UPI Safety (Specific to Indian College Students)

7. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, NPCI, or college administration. Legitimate organizations will never ask for these.

8. **Understand the difference between "Receive Money" and "Pay."** A UPI collect request always asks you to PAY. If someone says "I'm sending you money, just click approve" — it is a SCAM. Approving sends your money to them.

9. **Verify the recipient's UPI ID carefully** before any transfer, especially when buying from unknown sellers on OLX or social media. One wrong character in a UPI ID redirects your money.

10. **Be skeptical of "too good to be true" offers** — cheap gadgets, instant job offers, or scholarship verifications that require you to "pay first" are almost always fraudulent.

## Reporting Cybercrime in India

If you are a victim of any cybercrime or online fraud:

- 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline Number:** **1930** (available 24x7)
- 🏛️ **Nearest Cyber Police Station** or local police station

**Report immediately** — the faster you report a UPI fraud, the higher the chance of blocking the transaction or recovering funds.
