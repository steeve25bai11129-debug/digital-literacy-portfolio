## Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud Targeting College Students

### Type of Cybercrime
**UPI / Online Payment Fraud**

---

### What Is This Crime?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of financial cybercrime where attackers deceive victims into authorizing fraudulent transactions through India's digital payment ecosystem. Unlike traditional bank fraud, UPI scams exploit the simplicity and speed of instant payments — and the victim's own trust — to steal money within seconds.

---

### How It Typically Happens – Step by Step

1. **Initial Contact:** The attacker contacts the victim via WhatsApp, Instagram DM, or phone call, often posing as a classmate, online seller, or government official.

2. **Creating a Pretext:** The scammer fabricates an urgent scenario — for example, claiming to be a senior student selling second-hand textbooks, a college admin asking to verify a scholarship payment, or a buyer overpaying on OLX who needs a "refund."

3. **Sending a Collect Request:** Instead of sending money, the attacker sends a UPI "collect money" request disguised as a payment. The victim sees an amount and a "Pay" button — but clicking it sends money OUT of their account, not into it.

4. **Urgency and Pressure:** The attacker pressures the victim to act fast: "The offer expires in 2 minutes," or "Your scholarship will be cancelled if you don't verify now."

5. **OTP Manipulation:** In some cases, the attacker calls pretending to be bank support, asking the victim to share their OTP to "confirm a refund." Sharing the OTP gives the attacker full access to authorize the transaction.

6. **Money Transferred:** Once the victim clicks Pay or shares the OTP, the money is instantly debited. UPI transactions are irreversible once completed.

---

### Who Is Usually Targeted?

- College students buying/selling second-hand items on OLX, Quikr, or social media
- Students expecting scholarship or stipend disbursements
- First-year students unfamiliar with how UPI collect requests work
- Students looking for part-time jobs or freelance gigs online

---

### Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (amounts ranging from ₹500 to ₹50,000+)
- Emotional distress, embarrassment, and loss of trust
- Potential sharing of personal banking credentials leading to further exploitation
- Academic disruption due to stress and financial hardship
- Difficulty recovering funds as UPI transactions are instant and hard to reverse
