# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**Prepared by:** Steeve Rex | 25BAI11129 | VIT Bhopal
**Course:** Digital Literacy (NGMC) | Task 4 – Part B

---

## ✅ DO's – Responsible Social Media Use

1. **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile picture and a bio that reflects your academic and career goals.
2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or a future employer saw this?"
3. **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review and update who can see your posts, photos, and personal information.
4. **Give credit when sharing** – Always attribute content (articles, images, ideas) to the original creator.
5. **Engage positively** – Comment constructively, support peers, and participate in academic discussions online.
6. **Use academic and professional networks** – Maintain updated profiles on LinkedIn, GitHub, and ResearchGate for career opportunities.
7. **Verify before sharing** – Check the credibility of news or information before sharing it to avoid spreading misinformation.
8. **Report harmful content** – Use platform reporting tools to flag harassment, fake accounts, or inappropriate content.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts – What to Avoid

1. **Don't share personal details publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or bank details on any social platform.
2. **Don't engage with or spread cyberbullying** – Never mock, harass, or publicly shame classmates or others online.
3. **Don't post confidential academic material** – Do not share exam papers, answer keys, or internal institutional communications.
4. **Don't use social media during exams or important classes** – It is both a distraction and a potential academic integrity violation.
5. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers blindly** – Verify the identity of people before connecting, especially on professional platforms.
6. **Don't post under the influence of strong emotions** – Avoid posting when angry, upset, or frustrated — such posts can damage relationships and reputation.
7. **Don't plagiarize or misrepresent your work** – Never claim someone else's content, code, or writing as your own.
8. **Don't overshare your location** – Avoid tagging your real-time location in posts, especially late at night or in unfamiliar places.
